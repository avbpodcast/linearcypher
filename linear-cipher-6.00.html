How it works & safety notes

Your passphrase is stretched into a 256-bit key with Argon2id (64 MiB, 3 passes), a memory-hard function that makes offline guessing extremely slow. The salt comes from your first word, so precomputed guessing tables cannot be shared across conversations. Each message is encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a fresh random nonce, then the encrypted bytes are rendered into a 256-symbol alphabet, or into ordinary letters starting with LC5. when the Letters output is chosen (useful for e-mail and SMS, which sometimes damage unusual symbols). Decryption reverses this and verifies the message was not altered.

Good to know: anyone with the passphrase can read and forge messages, so share it privately and pick a strong one (use Generate). This protects messages in transit and at rest; it cannot protect a device that is already compromised. Runs fully offline. No data ever leaves this page.
